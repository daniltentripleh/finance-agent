## Writing Style Guidelines

### Lead with Numbers (CRITICAL)

✓ **CORRECT**: "Revenue increased 150% YoY to $250M in Q4 2024, driven by..."
✗ **INCORRECT**: "The company saw strong revenue growth this quarter..."

### Professional Writing Standards

- **Front-load**: Most important information first
- **Data-driven**: Lead with numbers and metrics
- **Specific**: Concrete examples, not generic statements
- **Objective**: Present facts, acknowledge risks
- **Active voice**: "We estimate revenue will reach $500M"

### Number Formatting

- Billions: $X.XB (e.g., "$2.5B")
- Millions: $XXXM (e.g., "$250M")
- Always specify: YoY, QoQ, CAGR
- Basis points for small margin changes: "500bps"
- Year format: "2024A" (actual), "2025E" (estimate)

### Use "vs." not "versus"

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Rewriting Task 1 content**: Use almost verbatim - just reformat and add charts
2. **Sparse pages**: Every page must have BOTH text AND visuals
3. **Grouping charts at end**: Charts must be interspersed throughout text
4. **Writing in markdown**: Use DOCX format, NOT markdown
5. **Skipping Page 1 format**: Must follow exact institutional format
6. **Short sections**: Must meet minimum word counts
7. **Thin assumptions**: Projection Assumptions MUST be 2,000-3,000 words
8. **Vague scenarios**: Must have specific parameters for Bull/Base/Bear
9. **Plain text URLs**: All citations must be clickable hyperlinks
10. **Missing statistical summary**: Comps table must have max/75th/median/25th/min
11. **Charts not embedded**: All 25-35 charts must be IN document
12. **Numbers don't match model**: Verify all figures against source
